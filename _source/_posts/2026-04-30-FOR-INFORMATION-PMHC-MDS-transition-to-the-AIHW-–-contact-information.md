#### Primary Mental Health Care Minimum Data Set Transition to the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare – Contact Information

As part of the transition of the management of the Primary Mental Health Care Minimum
Data Set (PMHC MDS) from the Department of Health, Disability and Ageing (the
department) to the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW), the department is
providing updated contact information to support PHNs during this change.
AIHW is an independent statutory government agency that manages key national data
collections and reports on health and welfare data for the Australian community, including
in the mental health area.

Many PHNs will already be familiar with the work they have been progressing over the past
year, including re‑establishing the PMHC Data Reference Group (PMHC DRG) and conducting
a comprehensive review of the PMHC MDS. This review has involved extensive consultation
with PHNs, provider organisations, system vendors and other key stakeholders. The insights
gathered through this process will help shape future improvements to the dataset.

The transition of PMHC MDS management is progressing smoothly. AIHW is steadily taking
on additional roles and responsibilities and has developed a detailed understanding of the
technical, operational and governance elements of the PMHC MDS. This staged approach
positions AIHW well to support PHNs and to guide ongoing system improvements.

##### PMHC MDS related support {#pmhc-mds-related-support-2026x04x30}

To support the transition, AIHW has established a dedicated PMHC MDS team inbox
([pmhc.mds@aihw.gov.au](mailto:pmhc.mds@aihw.gov.au)). Please direct all data-related queries to this inbox in the first
instance. The AIHW team will be your primary point of contact moving forward and will
coordinate with the department as required.

This includes queries such as advice on whether particular programs, activities or funding
streams are in scope for PMHC MDS data collection, queries about the data model,
clarification of how programs should be represented or categorised within the PMHC MDS,
questions about mapping local program arrangements to PMHC MDS program types or
service activity, and guidance on how changes to programs or service models may affect
PMHC MDS reporting. The AIHW will also continue to manage the PMHC DRG.

The department will continue to oversee governance and policy matters relating to the
PMHC MDS and will work closely with AIHW to ensure a smooth and well-supported
transition for PHNs.

##### PHN MHSP funding related queries {#phn-mhsp-funding-related-queries-2026x04x30}

Please continue to direct queries related to PHN mental health and suicide prevention
program funding to the Mental Health and Suicide Prevention Division PHN Program and
Policy team inbox ([MHPHNRPC@Health.gov.au](mailto:MHPHNRPC@Health.gov.au)).

##### PMHC MDS technical system support {#pmhc-mds-technical-system-support-2026x04x30}

Logicly’s PMHC Help Desk ([support@pmhc-mds.com](mailto:support@pmhc-mds.com)) remains available to support PHNs
with technical system matters as per current arrangements. This includes:

* Assistance with system access and user accounts
* Data upload and submission issues
* Deletion or suppression of inactive provider organisation information
* Validation or error messages generated by the PMHC MDS system
* System functionality or performance issues
* Technical user training, including user management, data upload and data entry
* Troubleshooting technical problems encountered when using the PMHC MDS platform.
